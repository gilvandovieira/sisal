# Sisal v0.13+ roadmap — DuckDB / External OLAP Engine Investigation

DuckDB is attractive — but only **after** the analytical IR exists. v0.13+ is an
**investigation** of DuckDB (and other OLAP engines) as a later **compiler /
execution target** for the analytics IR built in
[v0.7](v0.7.0-roadmap.md)/[v0.8](v0.8.0-roadmap.md)/[v0.11](v0.11.0-roadmap.md).
It is explicitly **not** the first analytics milestone and is **not a
requirement** for any earlier ETL/analytics work.

## Goal

Determine whether, and how, the Sisal analytics IR should compile to DuckDB (and
similar engines) for analytical workloads the Postgres-first path serves poorly
— without making DuckDB a dependency of the core stack.

## Scope — investigate

- **DuckDB as an analytical execution backend** (in-process OLAP).
- **DuckDB as a compiler target** from the existing analytics IR (the IR's
  `executionPreference` already anticipates this seam — see v0.7).
- **Querying external / local data** (files, attached databases).
- **Parquet / Arrow** possibilities (columnar in/out).
- **Extracting from Postgres/Neon into analytical execution** (read OLTP →
  analyze in DuckDB) — **ELT**-style workflows.
- **Performance benchmarks** vs. Postgres pushdown on representative analytics.
- **Deno compatibility** of DuckDB bindings; **native binding risks**;
  **deployment tradeoffs** (binary size, cold start, serverless viability).

## Why it comes after the IR (not before)

The analytics IR is the abstraction that lets Sisal target _more than one_
engine. Building DuckDB-first would couple analytics to one engine and bypass
the Postgres-first model most apps need. The IR (v0.8) must be the source of
truth; DuckDB is one of its backends.

## Non-goals

DuckDB is **not** required for v0.10 (ETL) or v0.11 (analytics). No DuckDB
dependency is added to `@sisal/core`/`orm`/adapters. No commitment to ship a
DuckDB backend — this milestone may conclude "not yet / not worth it."

## Risks

- **Native binding fragility on Deno** — DuckDB bindings may be immature or
  FFI-heavy; a hard dependency could hurt portability (and the Node/npm reach
  from v0.6).
- **Two-engine semantics drift** — analytics that behave differently on Postgres
  vs DuckDB. The IR + capability gates must contain this.
- **Premature optimization** — adopting DuckDB without benchmark evidence of a
  real Postgres-pushdown shortfall.

## Open questions

- Is DuckDB an **embedded** backend, an **attached** analytical store, or an
  **ELT scratch** engine? (Could be all three; pick the first use case.)
- Does compiling to DuckDB need the **transformable AST** deferred in v0.8, or
  can it compile from the same fragment IR with a DuckDB dialect?
- Parquet/Arrow: in scope here, or a separate ingestion concern (v0.14+)?
- Serverless/Deploy viability of a native engine.

## Acceptance criteria

- A written **DuckDB investigation report**: feasibility, IR-compilation sketch,
  benchmark results vs Postgres pushdown, Deno/Node binding risk assessment, and
  a go/defer/reject recommendation per use case.
- A prototype (behind a flag, not shipped) compiling one analytics IR query to
  DuckDB **only if** the benchmarks justify continuing.
- A clear statement that no earlier milestone depends on DuckDB.

## Affected packages

Investigation against `@sisal/core`/`@sisal/analytics` (a potential future
`@sisal/duckdb` backend); no required changes to existing packages.

## Required examples / tests

- Benchmark harness comparing an analytics query on Postgres pushdown vs DuckDB.
- A prototype IR→DuckDB compilation fixture (flagged), if pursued.

## Dialect compatibility notes

DuckDB would be a **new dialect/execution target** behind the analytics IR's
`executionPreference`, not a replacement for the pg/neon/sqlite/libsql adapters.
The Postgres-first analytics path remains the default.
